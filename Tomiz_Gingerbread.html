Gingerbread Cookies

Description

Gingerbread cookies are a simple treat made with ginger and cinnamon. Around Christmas time, families in Europe will bake these sweets, as it is said that the spices they contain ward off illness. They can be shaped freely, into stars and trees, not to mention people - hense the name "gingerbread man"

Source

Ingredients

  • Meiji Butter (Unsalted)
  • Powdered Sugar
  • Guerande Salt (Fine Grain)
  • 20g - Whole Egg (20g)
  • Cake Flour (Dolce, Ebetsu Milling)
  • Blanched Almond Powder
  • Organic Ginger (Powder)
  • Organic Cinnamon (Powder)
  • Organic Nutmeg (Powder)

Steps

  1. Cookie Dough
  2. Mix softened butter, powdered sugar, and salt with a rubber spatula.
  3. Add the whole egg in several batches, mixing each time.
  4. Add the sifted dry ingredients and mix with a rubber spatula until the dough is no longer powdery.
  5. Place the dough between OPP sheets, set a ruler, and roll it out with a rolling pin to a thickness of 3mm.
  6. Chill the dough in the freezer until it is firm enough to cut out shapes easily.
  7. Use cookie cutters to cut the dough and place the shapes on a baking sheet lined with a sheet for better baking results.
  8. *If the dough becomes too soft and hard to cut, chill it in the freezer again.
  9. Baking
  10. Preheat the oven to 150°C (302°F) and bake for about 15-18 minutes. Once baked, cool the cookies on the baking sheet over a cake cooler until they reach room temperature.
  11. Icing
  12. Mix powdered sugar with dried egg whites.
  13. Add water and whisk until the mixture turns white and thick. Place the icing in a piping bag (or a makeshift cone) and seal the top with tape.
  14. *For how to make a piping bag (cone), please refer to this guide.
  15. Finishing
  16. Use the icing in the piping bag to decorate the cookies.
  17. Let the icing dry at room temperature, and your cookies are ready!
